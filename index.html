Quantum Chemistry

The Basic Idea

All the theory that is give after newtons laws of motion is to explain may body systems in some way or the other. Quantum theory is also one of the approach to do such things. The prime reason for this theory to capture the concept of wave particle duality simultaneously. In a single mathematical equation. Its equations is comes from Hamiltonian mechanics. Hamiltonian mechanics could thought of as a mathematical model that trys to explain the motion of some though interacting with each other but immiscible fluids. This model does this thing very picularly. It does so by equating the energy descriptor of a quantity called wave function \(\Psi\) . Which is described as a quantity with no physical significance but when you square it ( \(\Psi^{2}\) ) you will get the probability of finding that particle in a given region. Although the quantity \(\Psi\) is not thought of as is prior to its derivation. It is the solution of the differntial equation that came out so that its explaination would be that. The differential equation is called Shordinger equation.


Shordinger equation

Time-independent Schrödinger equation

\[ \left[\frac{-\hbar^{2}}{2m} \nabla^{2} + V (\mathbf{r})\right] \Psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\Psi(\mathbf{r}) \] In most simple explaination of this equation is total energy \(E\)is equale to the sum of potential energy \(V (\mathbf{r})\) and kinetic energy \(\frac{ -\hbar^{2}}{2m} \nabla^{2} \). The fancy looking term is just similar term to square of velocity. In Chemistry mostly Time-independent Schrödinger equation to explain consistance properties of material. There are various case for which this equation is solved. Depending on the system one is studying. For chemists The most important system is Hydrogen atom. Also this is most complex system that can be solved compleatly. All the other system have just approximate solution based on Hydrogen atom solution.


Hydrogen Atom

Hydrogen atom Schrödinger equation

\[ \left( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\mu} \nabla^{2} - \frac{e^{2}}{4\pi \epsilon_{0} r}\right) \psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\psi(\mathbf{r}) \]

Its solution is

\[ \psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\phi) = \sqrt{ \left ( \frac{2}{na_{0}^{*}}\right)^{2} \frac{(n-l-1)!}{2n(n+l)!} } e^{-\rho/2} \rho^{l} L^{2l+1}_{n-l-1}(\rho) Y^{m}_{l}(\theta,\phi) \] where: \[ \rho = \frac{2r}{na_{0}^{*}} \] \( L^{2l + 1}_{n-l-1}(\rho) \)is a generalized Laguerre polynomial of degree \( n-l-1\)
\( Y^{m}_{l}(\theta,\phi) \) is a spherical harmonic function of degree \(l\) and order \(m\).
In this solution we get 3 quantum number \(n,l,m\) ,which give rise various kind of functions describing all allowed wave function of electron that can exist in hydrogen. For solution further systems combination of these wave function is used.
